This page is for first-time agency teams running a short non-operational AlphaRelay rehearsal. Use it to validate devices, roles, LAN relay, event logging, review, report drafting, and offline export before field use.

This checklist is a practical relay-mission rehearsal, enough steps to validate live video, synced events from two sides, and post-flight review. The pilot app runs on approved Android tablets or phones; the command side is any laptop or workstation with a browser.

## Before you fly (once)

- Sign in at [alpha-relay.com](https://www.alpha-relay.com). Under **Account**, save **full name** and **rank/title** so mission and custody attribution look right later.
- Download the pilot app from [alpha-relay.com/download.html](https://www.alpha-relay.com/download.html), install it, grant permissions when prompted, and step through the **First-Time Guide** on first launch.
- Activate the bridge license (**Organization** gives the QR code or pasteable `ar-…` key). Long-pressing **Choose Mission Mode** opens license settings anytime without starting a mission.
- Optional but useful: once signed in from the workstation browser, use **Install app** in Mission Overwatch so the shell can reopen after brief offline stretches (sign-in and cloud sync still need the network).

## Live test (minimal)

1. Preflight as your agency requires: aircraft and controller powered, pilot tablet/phone on the RC, **command laptop on the same Wi‑Fi subnet** as the pilot device for this exercise.
2. On the pilot: **Choose Mission Mode** → optional mission name/scenario → **Relay mission**. Make sure licensing does not block the start.
3. Give the workstation and pilot a moment online so **Active missions** on the AlphaRelay **dashboard** picks up the new mission (metadata includes the relay LAN hints for the tablet).
4. On the workstation: click **Open in console** for that mission so Mission Overwatch opens already focused (`?mission=…`).
5. Let the relay attempt complete automatically once the synced IP fills in. Only if the feed does **not** come up: trust the relay certificate if the browser prompts, retry **Connect**, leave **Tablet/phone IP (LAN)** empty and scan, or paste the IPv4 shown on the pilot—see [Mission setup](/alpharelay/mission-setup).
6. Confirm you see meaningful live feed or telemetry HUD text (Exact visuals depend on aircraft state and relay health).

## Logging and shutdown

- Log at least one pilot quick tap (buttons or wheel) and either a Mission Overwatch quick button **or** a short **Manual Event (fast)** note so both sides hit the timeline.
- Keep the flight short and within DJI and local policy—this pass is about workflow, not duration.
- **Land** cleanly, then tap **Stop Mission** on the pilot to end relay + mission together. If bandwidth is flaky, skim the pilot/handoff banners for queued footage before disconnecting Wi‑Fi.

## Wrap-up (optional smoke test)

- After the pilot or browser catches up online, confirm footage status (uploaded versus queued) before deep review—see [Footage upload](/alpharelay/footage-upload) when something looks stuck.
- Open **Play with events**, scrub briefly, confirm event markers align; add one **Tag event** if you want extra confidence in evidence capture.
- **Generate Report** for the closed mission (and **Submit for supervisor approval** only if testing that role).
- **Export Offline Package** if you must prove chain-of-handoff/offline readability.

Field-device mission rehearsals (no LAN relay assumptions) or teams that rely solely on remote **Live stream (WebRTC)** should read [Mission setup](/alpharelay/mission-setup) and [Pilot app and local AI](/alpharelay/pilot-app-and-local-ai) alongside this list.
