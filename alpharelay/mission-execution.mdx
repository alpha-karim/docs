This page is for Android field operators running the mission once setup is complete. Use it during flight to understand AlphaRelay controls, controller-first flying, tap-to-fly, and local AI event support.

## What happens if internet drops?

AlphaRelay is built for imperfect field networks. Local mission work can continue when supported by the current mission mode. Events and footage can queue locally, and cloud sync resumes when connectivity returns. LAN relay, WebRTC remote live, and cloud sync are separate paths, so losing one connection type does not always mean the entire mission stops.

## Takeoff

On the pilot tablet or phone, tap **Takeoff**, then follow all standard DJI safety steps.

## Flight and camera controls

During flight, use the on-screen pilot controls to adjust:

- Altitude
- Yaw / rotation
- Gimbal angle
- Camera positioning
- Obstacle avoidance and related DJI controls when available

Teams that prefer flying with only the physical controller can simplify the overlay. Quick events and telemetry remain available.

## Tap-to-fly

To use tap-to-fly:

1. Touch and hold on the live video feed.
2. Hold in the direction you want the aircraft to move.
3. Release to stop movement.

AlphaRelay maps the touch position to DJI virtual-stick pitch and roll. The aircraft must be airborne with telemetry available, and the pilot can choose slow, medium, or fast tap-to-fly speed in the pilot app.

## Local AI during flight

If your team enables local AI in the pilot app, on-device detection samples the same live relay frames used by Mission Overwatch. Confirmed detections can create local AI events with screenshots and metadata.

Use local AI as an assistive signal only. Review auto-tagged events the same way you review pilot quick events and voice relay entries.
