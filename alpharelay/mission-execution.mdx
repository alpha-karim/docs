## What this is

This page explains the field controls available during a mission after setup is complete.

## When to use it

- You are the Android field operator.
- You need to understand takeoff, landing, camera, or tap-to-fly controls.
- Your team wants to fly mostly from the physical controller.
- You need to know where optional local AI fits.

## Basic steps

1. Start the mission from the Android field app.
2. Tap **Takeoff**, then follow standard DJI safety steps.
3. Use the on-screen controls only when appropriate for your workflow.
4. Log important events during the mission.
5. Tap **Land**, then follow normal landing and safety procedures.
6. Tap **Stop Mission** when the mission is complete.

## What good looks like

- The pilot follows normal aircraft and agency safety procedures.
- Mission events are captured as they happen.
- Footage recording or handoff follows the documented workflow.
- The mission is closed cleanly before review begins.

## Common problems

- On-screen controls are left visible when the pilot prefers the physical controller.
- Local AI events are treated as final facts without review.
- The app is closed before upload or queued sync can finish.

## More detail

During flight, the Android field app can support altitude, yaw or rotation, gimbal angle, camera positioning, and related DJI controls when available. Pilots who prefer the physical controller can use the app option to hide on-screen drone controls while keeping quick events, telemetry, and relay status visible.

To use tap-to-fly, touch and hold on the live video feed, hold in the direction you want the aircraft to move, and release to stop movement. The aircraft must be airborne with telemetry available. The pilot can choose slow, medium, or fast tap-to-fly speed in the app.

If your team enables local AI, on-device detection can create local AI events with screenshots and metadata. Use local AI as an assistive signal only. Review auto-tagged events the same way you review other mission events.
