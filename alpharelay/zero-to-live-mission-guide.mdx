This guide walks operators through the complete AlphaRelay workflow, from initial setup to running a live mission, uploading footage, reviewing events, and generating an after-action report.

## 1. One-time setup

### 1.1 Open AlphaRelay in the browser

Go to [https://www.alpha-relay.com](https://www.alpha-relay.com), then sign in using your operator credentials.

### 1.2 Install the AlphaRelay pilot app

Download the Android APK from [https://www.alpha-relay.com/download.html](https://www.alpha-relay.com/download.html).

After downloading:

1. Install and open the app.
2. Grant the requested permissions, including camera, microphone, storage, location, and any other required access.
3. Complete the first-run onboarding steps.
4. Activate the pilot license.

To activate the license:

1. In AlphaRelay, go to **Organization** → **Pilot Tablet / Android Bridge License**.
2. Use the QR code or license key issued by your admin.
3. Scan or enter the license inside the pilot app when prompted.

You can also update the license later through the pilot app’s AlphaRelay license flow.

## 2. Mission setup

### 2.1 Network and hardware

Before starting a mission:

- Put the pilot tablet and command device on the same Wi-Fi network.
- Connect the pilot tablet to the drone controller.
- Power on the drone controller.
- Power on the aircraft.

The command device can be a laptop, desktop, another tablet or phone, or the same device running the app.

### 2.2 Start the relay and mission console

**On the pilot tablet**

1. Tap **Start Relay + DJI**.
2. Complete any license step if prompted.

**In the web app**

1. From the home or command dashboard, click **Open Mission Console**.
2. Under **Mission**, enter the mission name.
3. Select a **Scenario Template**, if your team uses one.
4. Click **Start Mission**.
5. Under **Live Overwatch Screen**, enter the IPv4 address shown on the pilot tablet (for example, `192.168.1.224`).
6. Click **Connect**.

<Tip>
  You can also leave the IP field blank and use **Connect** or **Scan LAN**, depending on the on-screen hint.
</Tip>

If the browser asks you to trust the pilot relay certificate:

1. Click **Trust Relay Certificate**.
2. Complete the trust page in the new tab.
3. Return to Mission Overwatch.
4. Connect again.

## 3. Mission execution

### 3.1 Takeoff

On the pilot tablet, tap **Takeoff**, then follow all standard DJI safety steps.

### 3.2 Flight and camera controls

During flight, use the on-screen pilot controls to adjust:

- Altitude
- Yaw / rotation
- Gimbal angle
- Camera positioning

Teams that prefer flying with only the physical controller can simplify the overlay. Quick events and telemetry remain available.

### 3.3 Tap-to-fly

To use tap-to-fly:

1. Touch and hold on the live video feed.

The aircraft will move in that direction based on DJI tap-to-fly behavior. Supported directions include forward, back, left, and right.

## 4. Event logging

### 4.1 Logging events on the pilot tablet

On the pilot tablet, operators can log events in several ways:

- Use the quick event controls.
- Say “relay” when voice logging is enabled.
- Type a custom event line in the pilot control panel.

Quick event presets can match what Mission Overwatch displays for the selected scenario.

### 4.2 Logging events in Mission Overwatch

In the web console, use either **Event Logging Quick Buttons** or **Manual Event** → **Log Manual Event**.

### 4.3 What gets stored

Logged events are:

- Timestamped
- Added to the **Event Log**
- Associated with mission activity
- Linked to screenshots from the pilot view when the relay is connected

If the system is offline or delayed, events and screenshots may sync once connectivity returns.

## 5. Completing a mission

### 5.1 Land the aircraft

On the pilot tablet, tap **Land**, then follow normal landing and safety procedures.

### 5.2 Close the mission

In the Mission Console, go to the Mission card and click **Close Mission**. This ends the mission and drops the live relay session.

## 6. Footage upload

### 6.1 Auto upload or manual upload

After the mission, the pilot app may attempt to automatically upload the recorded footage.

For auto upload to work, the pilot tablet must be connected to a network with internet access. Automatic upload can take time and may continue using the drone, controller, or tablet battery while the file is transferring.

If you do not want to wait for auto upload, or if battery life is a concern, tap **Skip**, then manually upload the footage later.

**To manually upload the file**

1. Remove the SD card from the aircraft or connect the aircraft to a computer.
2. Copy the mission MP4 file to your computer.
3. Open the mission in AlphaRelay.
4. Go to **Mission Footage**.
5. Click **Upload MP4**.
6. Select the correct video file.

Use auto upload when you have a reliable internet-connected network and enough battery remaining. Use manual upload when you need to preserve battery, leave the field quickly, or upload the file from a computer later.

If you see **Queued for cloud sync** or **Loading playback link**, stay signed in and online. The footage may take time to finish uploading or linking after the flight.

## 7. Footage playback

Uploaded clips appear under **Mission Footage**. From there, operators can:

- Watch footage using the built-in video player
- Play, pause, and scrub through clips
- Save a copy using the browser’s normal save or download options
- Open the clip with timeline events using **Play with Events**

There is a separate download button on each clip.

### 7.1 Play footage with events

Use **Play with Events** to open the timeline view with event markers, synced data, mission timestamps, and related logged activity.

After the mission is closed, use **Play with Events** in the sticky header for that past mission.

### 7.2 Evidence controls

All AlphaRelay missions include evidence controls for managing mission footage and maintaining a clear review record.

In the **Mission Footage** area, operators can use:

- Custody and integrity controls
- Cloud evidence controls
- Primary footage selection
- Multi-angle sync tools

These tools help teams organize footage, identify the primary video source, align multiple angles, and support a clear chain-of-custody workflow for mission review and reporting.

## 8. Report generation

### 8.1 Generate the after-action report

1. Scroll to **After-Action Report**.
2. Click **Generate Report**.

AlphaRelay uses Alpha AI to build the after-action report from the mission record, including events, screenshots, timestamps, mission metadata, scenario information, footage blocks, and custody and integrity data.

The report is generated from the structured mission timeline, helping turn live mission activity into a clear, review-ready after-action report.

### 8.2 Review and edit the report

After Alpha AI generates the report, AlphaRelay displays a structured after-action report that may include sections such as:

- Narrative summary
- Important timeline
- Evidence notes
- Items requiring officer review

The officer should carefully review the full report before finalizing it. Pay special attention to **Items Requiring Officer Review**. These are areas Alpha AI has identified as needing human confirmation, clarification, or additional detail.

The officer should:

- Review each item listed under **Items Requiring Officer Review**.
- Confirm whether the report accurately reflects the mission.
- Edit the report as needed.
- Add any missing context, corrections, or officer observations.
- Verify that the timeline, evidence notes, and mission summary are accurate.

The report should remain in draft form until the officer has reviewed and addressed any required items.

### 8.3 Seal the mission evidence

Once the report and evidence have been reviewed, the officer can seal the mission evidence. Sealing the mission locks the mission evidence and prevents further changes to the sealed evidence record.

Under **Mission Chain of Custody**, the officer may see options such as:

- **Download Mission Custody Certificate (HTML)**
- **Seal Entire Mission**
- **Seal Entire Mission and Verify Downloads**

Use these controls when the mission record is ready to be preserved.

**Before sealing, make sure**

- The correct footage is uploaded
- Events are accurate
- The report has been reviewed
- Items requiring officer review have been addressed
- Any needed edits have been completed
- The custody record is ready to be locked

Once the mission is sealed, evidence changes are no longer allowed.

### 8.4 Submit for supervisor approval or return to draft

After reviewing the report and evidence, the officer can choose the next step.

**Submit for supervisor approval**

Click **Submit for Supervisor Approval** when the mission report is complete and ready for command or supervisory review. Do this after:

- The report has been edited and verified
- Items requiring officer review have been addressed
- Evidence has been reviewed
- The mission record is ready for approval

**Return to draft**

Click **Return to Draft** if more changes are needed. Use **Return to Draft** when:

- The report needs additional edits
- Evidence notes need correction
- Officer review items still need to be addressed
- The mission record is not ready for supervisor approval

This allows the officer to continue working on the report before final submission.

## 9. Export offline package

### 9.1 What the offline package is

The **Export Offline Package** creates a downloadable ZIP file that contains a complete point-in-time copy of the mission record.

This package is designed for archival, handoff, and offline review. It allows the mission to be reviewed later without needing to access AlphaRelay’s servers.

The offline package may include:

- Mission record
- Chain-of-custody data
- Frozen custody certificate
- After-action report HTML file
- Footage clips, when available
- Event screenshots, when available
- A small offline viewer for reviewing the mission timeline and media

Footage and screenshots are placed inside a `media/` folder when the browser is able to download them.

### 9.2 How to export the offline package

1. Open the mission console.
2. Select the closed mission you want to export.
3. Confirm that the sticky header shows **Past Mission:**.
4. Click **Export Offline Package**.
5. If your browser asks where to save the file, choose a folder.
6. Wait for the export to complete. When the button shows a **Done** message, the ZIP file has been created.

Large missions may take longer because video files are streamed into the ZIP package during export. Chrome or Edge is recommended for very large missions because they can better support streaming large ZIP files directly to disk.

### 9.3 What is inside the ZIP

The offline package may include the following files and folders:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `index.html` | The local offline viewer. Open this file to review the mission package in a browser. |
| `viewer.js` and `viewer.css` | Files used by the offline viewer to display the mission timeline, footage, screenshots, and related data. |
| `mission.json` | The mission data file, including the mission record and offline paths to any included media. |
| `chain_of_custody.json` | The chain-of-custody data, including evidence rows, custody events, and validation summary. |
| `custody-certificate.html` | A printable custody certificate frozen at the time of export. |
| `after-action-report.html` | A local version of the after-action report. Open in a browser and print or save as a PDF. |
| `README.txt` | Instructions for opening and reviewing the offline package, including what to do if your browser blocks video playback from a local file. |
| `manifest.json` | A package index that lists the format of the export and the files included. |
| `media/` | Downloaded mission media when available, including footage clips and event screenshots. |

### 9.4 How to open the offline package

1. Unzip the downloaded file.
2. Open the extracted folder.
3. Double-click `index.html`.

That opens the offline mission viewer in your browser. From there, you can review the mission timeline, footage, screenshots, custody data, and report files included in the package.

### 9.5 Important note

The offline package is not a replacement for live mission sync. It is a point-in-time archive created while you are signed in and connected to the internet.

Use it for:

- Offline review
- Records retention
- Evidence handoff
- Supervisor or command review
- Archival storage
- Sharing a self-contained mission package when appropriate

Once exported, the package reflects the mission data available at the time the ZIP was created.

## Summary

With AlphaRelay, operators can move through the full mission lifecycle in one connected workflow:

**Setup → Live relay → Flight → Structured logging → Closeout → Footage upload → Event review → Alpha AI report generation → Officer review → Evidence sealing → Supervisor approval → Offline export**

AlphaRelay helps teams capture timestamped events, preserve mission context, attach screenshots, upload footage automatically or manually, review synchronized evidence, and generate structured after-action reports with Alpha AI.

Before final submission, officers can address any **Items Requiring Officer Review**, edit the report, confirm the mission timeline, verify the evidence record, and seal the mission to prevent further evidence changes.

After the mission is complete, teams can also export an offline mission package for records retention, handoff, command review, archival storage, or offline access. This package gives the team a self-contained copy of the mission record, including the report, custody data, certificate, timeline, and available media.

The result is a complete, review-ready mission package built for operational documentation, chain-of-custody support, supervisor approval, offline review, and long-term records.
