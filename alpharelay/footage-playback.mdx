Uploaded clips appear under **Mission Footage**. From there, operators can:

- Watch footage using the built-in video player
- Play, pause, and scrub through clips
- Save a copy using the browser’s normal save or download options
- Open the clip with timeline events using **Play with events**
- Review multiple uploaded angles from the same mission

There is a separate download button on each clip.

## Play footage with events

Use **Play with events** to open the timeline view with event markers, synced data, mission timestamps, and related logged activity.

After the mission is closed, the sticky header shows **Past mission:** with **Play with events**, **Export offline package…**, the executive summary, and **Full after-action report** when those actions apply.

The timeline viewer supports:

- Orange event markers on the scrubber
- Event screenshots in the side panel
- Timeline photos alongside mission events
- Active-event highlighting as the video plays
- Angle tabs for switching between primary and secondary clips
- **All angles** to tile every available feed in a synchronized view
- **Adjust sync to primary** on secondary clips, so teams can slide a secondary angle until it matches the primary drone timeline

Mission events stay tied to the primary mission timeline. When a secondary angle has a sync offset, AlphaRelay maps those same events onto the secondary video so review teams can inspect the same moment from another view.

## Tag post-mission events from footage

For closed missions, the Play with events viewer includes an **Event name** field and **Tag event** button. Use this when review reveals something important after the flight.

1. Open a closed mission.
2. Click **Play with events**.
3. Switch to the angle that shows the moment clearly.
4. Scrub to the frame you want to capture.
5. Enter a short event name.
6. Click **Tag event**.

AlphaRelay captures the current video frame as an event screenshot, adds the event to the mission timeline, and places a new marker on the footage timeline. If you leave the event name blank, AlphaRelay creates a default post-mission event label with the clip and time.

After a report is submitted for supervisor approval, timeline events and photos are locked until a supervisor returns the report to draft. Approved reports keep the timeline locked permanently.

## Evidence controls

All AlphaRelay missions include evidence controls for managing mission footage and maintaining a clear review record.

In the **Mission Footage** area, operators can use:

- Custody and integrity controls
- Cloud evidence controls
- Primary footage selection
- Multi-angle sync tools
- Mission photo custody controls

These tools help teams organize footage, identify the primary video source, align multiple angles, and support a clear chain-of-custody workflow for mission review and reporting.
