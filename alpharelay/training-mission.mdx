title: "First training mission"
description: "Run a safe rehearsal mission to teach the end-to-end AlphaRelay workflow."

---

Use a training mission before operational use. The goal is not flight complexity. The goal is to prove that the team understands the workflow.

## Recommended setup

- Use a non-operational training location.
- Keep the flight short.
- Use a simple scenario template.
- Assign one Android field operator and one Mission Overwatch operator.
- Assign one organization admin if you want to test supervisor approval.
- Keep a stable local Wi-Fi network available.
- Have internet access ready for post-mission sync and upload.

## Training script

1. On the pilot tablet or phone, tap **Choose Mission Mode**, pick a scenario in the overlay (or **No scenario template**), set the mission name to `Training mission`, and tap **Relay mission**.
2. On the command workstation, sign in at the AlphaRelay **Mission Dashboard** and click **Open in console** for the active mission. Confirm Mission Overwatch auto-loads relay info from synced metadata and attaches to the LAN relay (`Connect`/scan remains a troubleshooting fallback **only if** attachment fails).
3. Confirm live video and telemetry.
4. Log a pilot quick event.
5. Log a voice relay or manual pilot event if enabled.
6. Log a Mission Overwatch manual event.
7. Fly or record briefly using normal DJI safety procedures.
8. Tap **Land**.
9. Tap **Stop Mission** on the pilot tablet or phone to close the mission and end the relay session.
10. Upload the mission footage, or confirm pilot-handoff footage queues if the network is offline.
11. Upload one mission photo from Mission Overwatch.
12. Open **Play with events**.
13. Confirm the event markers appear on the timeline.
14. Add one post-mission **Tag event** from the video.
15. Generate the after-action report.
16. Submit, return, or approve the report if testing the supervisor flow.
17. Export the offline package.

## What the team should learn

After the training mission, every operator should be able to answer:

- Which device starts the relay and the mission?
- How does Mission Overwatch consume the relay after the dashboard hands off the focused mission (`Open in console`), and when do ops fall back to **Connect**, scan, or manual IPv4?
- Where do live events appear?
- What happens when internet is unavailable?
- How does footage get uploaded, and when can pilot-handoff footage queue?
- How do mission photos attach to the timeline or evidence record?
- How do reviewers use **Play with events**?
- Who can submit, return, or approve the after-action report?
- When should an officer seal the evidence record?
- What is included in an offline package?

## Pass criteria

The training mission is successful when:

- Mission Overwatch reaches the pilot relay reliably (automatic connection after dashboard hand-off, otherwise scan/manual recovery).
- At least two events appear in the event log.
- Mission footage appears, or pilot-handoff footage is queued for upload.
- **Play with events** opens and shows the event timeline.
- A post-mission tagged event appears in the event log.
- A mission photo appears in the mission record.
- The after-action report generates.
- The offline package exports.

Repeat this training workflow whenever the team changes devices, networks, aircraft, or operating procedures.
