This page is for agencies that need to understand what AlphaRelay processes locally, what syncs to the cloud, and what must be reviewed before sharing, sealing, approving, or exporting a mission record.

## What runs locally

The Android field device runs the pilot app, mission controls, quick events, relay status, and optional local AI event detection when configured.

Local event detection and tagging runs on-device. It supports field awareness and review, but it does not replace pilot judgment or officer review.

Mission Overwatch can also keep a local app shell after it has been opened online, so the page can reload in the same browser profile during low-connectivity work. Sign-in, sync, uploads, and playback links still need network access.

## What syncs to the cloud

Cloud sync can include mission metadata, event records, screenshots, mission photos, report data, custody records, uploaded footage references, review status, and organization context needed to support shared review.

Footage uploads occur when a connected device uploads the file. Depending on the workflow, that may be the Android field device, Mission Overwatch after a LAN handoff, or a reviewer manually uploading an MP4.

## AI processing

Alpha AI drafts after-action report material from structured mission data. It can use events, timestamps, mission metadata, screenshots, photos, footage references, custody context, and review state.

AI after-action report generation uses structured mission data and requires human review. Treat AI output as draft material, not final authority.

Operators must verify:

- Timeline accuracy.
- Evidence notes.
- Media references.
- Items Requiring Officer Review.
- Mission photos and captions.
- Custody and sealing state.

## Offline export packages

An offline package is a point-in-time archive created from the mission data and media that the browser can access during export.

The package can include the mission record, report, custody data, custody certificate, event screenshots, mission photos, footage clips, and an offline viewer.

Before sharing an offline package, verify that the mission has been reviewed, synced, sealed or approved as required by agency policy, and exported from the correct mission.

## Custody hashes and sealing

Custody hashes and sealing support evidentiary review by preserving records of reviewed files and custody evidence. Sealing is not the same as supervisor approval.

Sealing preserves custody evidence and hashes. Supervisor approval finalizes the after-action report and locks timeline/photos.

## Before sharing, approving, sealing, or exporting

- Confirm the correct mission is open.
- Confirm footage and screenshots are present.
- Resolve Items Requiring Officer Review.
- Review the Alpha AI draft as an officer-authored record.
- Confirm custody evidence is ready to preserve.
- Confirm supervisor approval is appropriate before making the report permanent.
- Confirm the offline package includes the records your agency needs.
